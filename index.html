Mahendra Singh Dhoni

One of the greatest cricket captains, wicket-keeper-batsman and finishers in the history of cricket.

About the Legend

☛ M S Dhoni, in full Mahendra Singh Dhoni. was born on on July 7, 1981, in Ranchi, he grew up with a passion for sports from a young age.

☛ He is popularly known as "Captain Cool," is an iconic cricketer whose impact on the sport and the hearts of cricket enthusiasts is unparalleled.

☛ Dhoni's rise to prominence began in 2004 when he made his ODI debut against Bangladesh.

☛ Dhoni took over the captaincy of the Indian cricket team in 2007, leading them to significant successes in various formats.

☛ Under Dhoni's leadership, India won the inaugural ICC T20 World Cup in 2007, beating Pakistan in a thrilling final.

☛ Dhoni's tenure as Test captain saw India reach the pinnacle of the ICC Test rankings for the first time in 2009.

☛ Dhoni's most significant achievement came when he captained India to victory in the ICC Cricket World Cup 2011, ending a 28-year drought.

☛ Known for his calm and composed demeanor on the field, Dhoni led by example and earned the respect of his teammates. Dhoni popularized the "helicopter shot," a unique and powerful stroke, which became his trademark.

☛ Throughout his career, Dhoni amassed numerous records, both as a batsman and a wicketkeeper.

☛He received several accolades, including the Rajiv Gandhi Khel Ratna ,India's highest sporting honor, in 2007 and Padma Bhushan awards,the third-highest civilian award in India, in the year 2018.

☛ Dhoni's success inspired a generation of cricketers, particularly from smaller towns and cities. Even after his retirement, Dhoni's legacy and impact on Indian cricket continue to be felt.

☛ Apart from cricket, Dhoni has been actively involved in various philanthropic endeavors, supporting causes close to his heart.
